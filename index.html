PANDEMONIUM!
I'll Answer That POI Via Notes

From the perspective of a 9th grader, at least in my experience, Model United Nation was petrifying. There you are, standing in front of maybe 50 others in a conference room getting ready to deliver your argument regarding the newly added amendment on the resolution paper. As you’re presenting, thoughts scramble through your head in all sorts of directions. You wondered if anyone will return it to you with a counter-argument that will leave you speechless. You wonder if your argument is even valid; that your research was solid and that you’re well prepared enough for the fusillade of POI (Points of Inquiry) that’s about to head your way. It is truly a mentally taxing experience, but one that I find to be extremely helpful in allowing me to develop other skills beyond the traditional classrooms.


It is at these conferences where I learned how to deliver strong arguments in a confident and concise manner, to communicate and work effectively with others to come up with resolutions to real world issues, and to build confidence in my research and presentation abilities.

UNIS
MUN Experience At UNIS Hanoi

UNISMUN was undoubtedly one of my most memorable MUN experiences at UNIS of Hanoi. As one of the most notable highschool MUN conference hosts, the competitiveness of the event was beyond that of any other I’ve been a part of at the time. Yet, for the same reason, it was also where I best improved as a delegate as a result of being able to learn so much from others.


UNIS was diverse; much more so than any other MUN environment or even international environment in Vietnam. Being at UNIS, despite it only being only a brief, was as eye opening as it was intellectually stimulating. I was able to learn from so many diverse perspectives about a myriad of different things. It was interesting to see how other delegates handled different situations and in regards to their country’s standings. Perhaps the most enriching thing about UNIS, and with many other MUN conferences, is that you’re exposed to an environment of extremely passionate and dedicated students who could very well be future leaders. While the debating sessions were as entertaining as they are, my favorite part was during the resolution process where most of the collaborative work and critical thinking takes place.

A TOUGH YEAR
Improvise, Adapt, Overcome

It is commonly believed, at the very least, in the US that your Junior (11th) year will be an arduous one; perhaps the most challenging considering that only the first semester of 12th grade will be evaluated.


In my case, that couldn’t be more true. Studying abroad, away from the comfort of your own home, certainly is challenging but it also has its perks. Initially, my experience was depressing: I had trouble socializing, and connecting with my peers. Many of my interests, values, and priorities were unrelatable with many. Finding the right group of people in a cluster of hundreds of new faces was overwhelming. However, getting accustomed to the extreme weather of Iowa, the people, and my new life was a rewarding journey, nonetheless, in regards to my character development. I developed to be more patient, more open-minded to novelty around me, and most importantly, the ability to adapt and overcome: all of which are traits that complimented my determination and ambitious nature. As a result, my Junior year became one of my best performing highschool year as well as being one of the most difficult.

IN PURSUIT OF KNOWLEDGE
The Importance Of Science

As a child growing up, I’ve always been exuberant about science. For a large portion of my middle school years, I was captivated by the field of astronomy. From the formation of Earth to extraterrestrial phenomena such as blackholes and antimatter, I’ve always been intrigued by the natural development of the universe and how minuscule we, humans, are to the relative universe as we know it.


On a much smaller and immediate scale, if we take a look around us we’ll notice that our current development as a species has been heavily enabled by our scientific discoveries and continual explorations in the STEM field. Later on, I was particularly invested in Biology and Biochemistry to which I started pre-learning AP biology as early as 9th grade. By 11th grade, I decided to enroll in 3 science classes: AP Chemistry, SLU Biology, and Honors Human Anatomy; all of which were classes that compliment each other in a way that allowed me to better comprehend the materials. The adrenaline and exhilaration out of connecting the dots and fully understanding the subject is unparalleled. For such a reason, regardless of technicality and complexity, learning new concepts now is just as exciting as my childhood space exploration days.

A BETTER ATHLETE
Xavier Wrestling Team

One of the biggest cultural shocks for me while transitioning to studying abroad was the sheer difference in the athleticism amongst students. The athletes at Xavier High School were superior in terms of stamina, strength, and size in comparison to the competition back in Vietnam. As a result, the competition was much fiercer and I was no longer able to play at the highest level of highschool basketball within my local region.


I decided to try for another sport where my competition would be those my size to which I found wrestling. Wrestling was a much more physically demanding sport than basketball. My experience as a member of the wrestling team was extremely laborious. Our training schedule was up to 5 sessions a week: each of which lasted for up to 3 hours of tackling one another to the floor, lifting weights, performing drills, learning techniques and stances, and a tremendous amount of cardio. The exercises would gradually become a routine and the routine would gradually turn into habits. It is thanks to wrestling that I am consistently lift-weights today. Although I didn’t manage to perform particularly well with most of my matches being losses, I left the team as the best athlete I’ve ever been.

MUN ABROAD
MUN Experience In The US

Upon setting foot within the States, one of the things I looked forward to was the MUN experience. I wonder how it would differ from that in Vietnam. Having heard about it from a couple friends, I was under the impression that it would be more challenging as conferences are much larger and hence would entail a higher level of competition.


However, despite the fact that ISHMUN was bigger in number of participants, it wasn't necessarily more competitive but rather the opposite. Delegates and chairs were more relaxed. We weren’t required to follow strict guidelines and speech protocols that were required in the conferences that I was a part of in Vietnam. Each conference had up to 100 people and each country was assigned with 2 delegates: in order words, I had a partner. In addition, the resolution drafting process was also different in a lot of ways where delegates will draft and debate simultaneously. Although there were a lot of differences, it was easier to manage as I had a partner to aid me through the process.


By speaking in front of a much larger, unfamiliar, crowd, I was able to build greater confidence in my public speaking abilities. The diversity in the group of participants has also enabled me to exchange with not only high school students within the local region but also from MUN enthusiasts from all across Iowa.

SECOND HOME
The Montessori Environment

Unlike any of my previous school year experience, my Senior year experience is unique in that it adheres to the Montessori method of education, hence the name of the school: New Gate Montessori IB. In short, the Montessori method places heavy emphasis on enabling students to develop the ability to independently learn and self initiate. As a result, not only were the roles and responsibilities of teachers and students different, but also the learning environment, grading system, etc.


Similarly to my transitioning experience between my sophomore year and Junior year, it was difficult at first to adjust to, once again, another environment. However as the year progressed, I gradually learned to enjoy the Montessori method. The relationship between students and teachers was more of a friendship. Students were encouraged to take their learning at their own pace. Independence was heavily emphasized and I enjoyed the freedom to learn in the manner that I wished. It was at New Gate that, for the first time, I felt connected with the teachers and my peers while studying abroad. Eventually, my perception of the school was no longer just any school but rather an environment for me to flourish academically and mature as an individual. It is this disintegration of the perception that New Gate was a school that empowered me to perform exceptionally well. Although I am proud of my perfect GPA, it is not the result that can be solely attributed to my work ethic, but rather to the teachers' relentless support and attentiveness, the competitiveness and dedication of my peers which ultimately serve as a source of motivation, and the welcoming and home-like atmosphere that New Gate exudes.

AN UNFORGETTABLE INTERNSHIP
Internship At Crosby Counseling Center

While the years leading up to my Senior years have been invigorating academically, I have yet to garner any work experience. As an aspiring entrepreneur at the time, I needed an opportunity to dive deeper into the field of digital marketing; an opportunity that could best serve as a learning environment while also giving me the freedom to experiment, engage in meaningful activities, and partake in the operations of an actual workplace.


Fortunately for me, I found the perfect opportunity to intern at Crosby Counseling Center: a relatively small counseling business. Crosby Counselling Center did not have a marketing team and their marketing efforts were lacklustering. Initially, I was given the role of an assistant office manager where I will be making callbacks, arranging the schedule for the counsellors and participating in meetings. As I familiarize myself with the industry by gaining a deeper understanding of the customers and the service that we provided, I wanted to revitalize their marketing strategy to which Dr.Crosby was comfortable with. Collecting consumer data for the kind of company that Crosby Counseling Center is was difficult due to the sensitive nature of the counselling work and the diversity in the counseling services. I was asked to, instead of establishing a full-fledged marketing campaign, start with setting a few marketing initiatives that were easier. Together with the counselors, we began running ad campaigns on social media and building our counselor profiles on sites such as Bark, Thervo, and Psychology Today.


At the time, what we were missing was a website to link to. The task of building a website was overwhelming and requires an extensive list of prerequisite skills to even begin building one. For that reason, I decided to learn wordpress, a content-management system, and build my very first website. The process of learning wordpress, consulting with the counselors regarding the content of the page, and putting a functional site together took me 2 months. The experience was amazing. I was given the opportunity to dabble in the field of digital marketing while taking real-world actions that could have positive/negative impacts. By devising my own strategy to stimulate the business’s marketing efforts, it was a very meaningful experience that allowed me to learn a lot from.
