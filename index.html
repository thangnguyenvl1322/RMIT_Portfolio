A PROMISE TO PERFORM
What Good Grades Mean

For the majority of my student life, I’ve always been highly achieving. For the first time, I wasn’t achieving enough to place myself on the honor roll. The immediate transition to highschool wasn’t so demanding. I managed fairly well in the first semester by placing myself on the Distinguished Honor Roll, but as the year progressed, the sheer abundance of distractions got the better of me. I find myself drifting away and indulging in pleasure far too often.


As a result, my inability to perform well became a wake-up call for me; a reminder of what I needed to focus on and what distractions to get rid of. I promise myself that I would not let an incident like this occur again. Perhaps to many, excellence in one’s academics is a reflection of their intellect. As I see it, it is more a reflection of one’s work ethic and determination rather than intellect. Getting outstanding grades for me was a form of validation; indicating that I’m capable of developing the work ethic and determination necessary to succeed in the future.

I'M BALLIN'
Growing Up With Basketball

Ever since childhood, I’ve always been a fanatic about basketball. It was my childhood dream to play in the NBA among the best players in the world. As a result, most of my childhood days were spent indulging in the sport; refining my dribbling and shooting skills in hope that one day, this basketball dream of mine would be realized. I’ve consistently been a part of the school’s basketball team since 5th grade, and it has been an extremely pleasant journey.


By the time, highschool was around, my schedule had become increasingly more hectic. As a result, I decided to join the B team instead of the A team in order to keep a good balance between my academics while still enabling myself to partake in playing the sport I grew up with. Being part of a sports team is always thrilling, and exciting. For that reason, I am more than excited to be exploring the athletic community present at RMIT University in the upcoming years of my studies.

PANDEMONIUM!
I'll Answer That POI Via Notes

From the perspective of a 9th grader, at least in my experience, Model United Nation was petrifying. There you are, standing in front of maybe 50 others in a conference room getting ready to deliver your argument regarding the newly added amendment on the resolution paper. As you’re presenting, thoughts scramble through your head in all sorts of directions. You wondered if anyone will return it to you with a counter-argument that will leave you speechless. You wonder if your argument is even valid; that your research was solid and that you’re well prepared enough for the fusillade of POI (Points of Inquiry) that’s about to head your way. It is truly a mentally taxing experience, but one that I find to be extremely helpful in allowing me to develop other skills beyond the traditional classrooms.


It is at these conferences where I learned how to deliver strong arguments in a confident and concise manner, to communicate and work effectively with others to come up with resolutions to real world issues, and to build confidence in my research and presentation abilities.

UNIS
MUN Experience At UNIS Hanoi

UNISMUN was undoubtedly one of my most memorable MUN experiences at UNIS of Hanoi. As one of the most notable highschool MUN conference hosts, the competitiveness of the event was beyond that of any other I’ve been a part of at the time. Yet, for the same reason, it was also where I best improved as a delegate as a result of being able to learn so much from others.


UNIS was diverse; much more so than any other MUN environment or even international environment in Vietnam. Being at UNIS, despite it only being only a brief, was as eye opening as it was intellectually stimulating. I was able to learn from so many diverse perspectives about a myriad of different things. It was interesting to see how other delegates handled different situations and in regards to their country’s standings. Perhaps the most enriching thing about UNIS, and with many other MUN conferences, is that you’re exposed to an environment of extremely passionate and dedicated students who could very well be future leaders. While the debating sessions were as entertaining as they are, my favorite part was during the resolution process where most of the collaborative work and critical thinking takes place.

A TOUGH YEAR
Improvise Adapt Overcome

It is commonly believed, at the very least, in the US that your Junior (11th) year will be an arduous one; perhaps the most challenging considering that only the first semester of 12th grade will be evaluated.


In my case, that couldn’t be more true. Studying abroad, away from the comfort of your own home, certainly is challenging but it also has its perks. Initially, my experience was depressing: I had trouble socializing, and connecting with my peers. Many of my interests, values, and priorities were unrelatable with many. Finding the right group of people in a cluster of hundreds of new faces was overwhelming. However, getting accustomed to the extreme weather of Iowa, the people, and my new life was a rewarding journey, nonetheless, in regards to my character development. I developed to be more patient, more open-minded to novelty around me, and most importantly, the ability to adapt and overcome: all of which are traits that complimented my determination and ambitious nature. As a result, my Junior year became one of my best performing years throughout my highschool experience as well as being one of the most difficult.


IN PURSUIT OF KNOWLEDGE
The Importance Of Science

As a child growing up, I’ve always been exuberant about science. For a large portion of my middle school years, I was captivated by the field of astronomy. From the formation of Earth to extraterrestrial phenomena such as blackholes and antimatter, I’ve always been intrigued by the natural development of the universe and how minuscule we, humans, are to the relative universe as we know it.


On a much smaller and immediate scale, if we take a look around us we’ll notice that our current development as a species has been heavily enabled by our scientific discoveries and continual explorations in the STEM field. Later on, I was particularly invested in Biology and Biochemistry to which I started pre-learning AP biology as early as 9th grade. By 11th grade, I decided to enroll in 3 science classes: AP Chemistry, SLU Biology, and Honors Human Anatomy; all of which were classes that compliment each other in a way that allowed me to better comprehend the materials. My academic performance in regard to these three classes earned me the Bausch + Lomb Honorary Science Award: a reflection of my genuine interest in science and one of my proudest academic achievements to date.


“If I have seen further it is by standing on the shoulders of giants” - Issac Newton
